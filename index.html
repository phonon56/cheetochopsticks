Why this exists

Public data should be readable
by the public.

The crash data was always public. The road funding numbers were always filed. The city's own Safety Action Plan said the right things. None of it was in a form that residents could actually use.

This is the attempt to fix that. No grant funding. No consulting contract. Two people, a kitchen table, USAA on hold, and a public ArcGIS endpoint that turned out to contain nine years of crash records. The data is the city's. We just built the door.

The offer on the table: Everything built here — the dashboard, the search tool, the analysis — is offered to the City of Colorado Springs for public use. No invoice attached. If it helps someone make a safer decision or ask a better question at a council meeting, that's the whole point.
