01

Our Commitment

El Paso County is committed to ensuring that all residents — including those with disabilities — can fully participate in the Land Development Code update process. This commitment is both a legal obligation under the Americans with Disabilities Act Title II and Colorado House Bill 21-1110, and a fundamental civic principle: public participation in a process that directly affects how land is developed in your community must be genuinely accessible to all.

The LDC update is one of the most consequential civic participation opportunities in El Paso County in the current decade. The code being written will govern development decisions for the next ten or more years. Excluding any resident from that process — including through inaccessible digital platforms — is a procedural failure the county is actively working to prevent.

If at any point you encounter a barrier to participation, we want to know. Contact information is provided throughout this statement. No one will be turned away from the process due to a disability.

02

Scope of This Statement

This accessibility statement covers the following digital surfaces associated with the El Paso County LDC update:

  • elpasocountyldc.com — the project website operated by El Paso County, listing events, documents, news, and participation opportunities.
  • elpasocountyldc.konveio.com — the public comment and document annotation platform operated by Konveio, a third-party vendor contracted by El Paso County.
  • Physical meeting accessibility — in-person community meetings, open houses, and public hearings related to the LDC update.
  • Document accessibility — draft LDC modules and supporting documents published for public review.

This statement does not cover third-party websites linked from this site (such as Municode, EDARP, or AgendaSuite), which have their own accessibility policies.

03

Technical Standard

El Paso County aims to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with disabilities, and are the standard referenced in:

  • ADA Title II Final Rule (28 C.F.R. Part 35) — published April 2024, effective for smaller public entities April 2026, requiring WCAG 2.1 Level AA for web content and mobile applications operated by public entities.
  • Colorado House Bill 21-1110 — state law providing civil enforcement rights for individuals with disabilities who experience discrimination due to inaccessible government technology.
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act — federal standard for electronic and information technology accessibility.
Plain Language

WCAG 2.1 Level AA covers four principles: content must be Perceivable (can be detected by any sense), Operable (can be navigated and used), Understandable (content and operation are clear), and Robust (works with assistive technologies). Level AA is the legally required minimum standard as of April 2026.

05

Known Limitations

The following limitations are currently known and are being actively addressed. This list will be updated as remediation progresses.

elpasocountyldc.com (Project Website)

  • Event list page (elpasocountyldc.com/event-list): The current Wix-hosted version of this page has interactive elements that may not be fully keyboard navigable. Users who rely on keyboard navigation should use the main navigation to access the event list, or contact plnweb@elpasoco.com for a text-based event listing.
  • PDF documents: Draft LDC modules and supporting documents may not be fully tagged for screen reader accessibility. Accessible versions (tagged PDF or Word format) are available on request at plnweb@elpasoco.com or 719-520-6300.

elpasocountyldc.konveio.com (Comment Platform)

  • Keyboard navigation: Some interactive annotation elements within the Konveio platform may not be fully accessible via keyboard alone, affecting users who cannot use a mouse or trackpad.
  • Screen reader compatibility: The document annotation interface may produce incomplete or confusing output for screen reader users, particularly when navigating between comment threads.
  • Focus management: Modal dialogs and comment submission forms may not return focus correctly after closing, disorienting screen reader users.
  • Color contrast: Some interface elements within the Konveio platform may not meet the 4.5:1 contrast ratio required at WCAG 2.1 Level AA for normal text.
Alternative Participation Path

If the Konveio platform is inaccessible to you, your comment still enters the official record through email. Send your written comment to plnweb@elpasoco.com with your name, address, and the specific section of the draft you are commenting on. This is a full equivalent alternative — your comment will be included in the administrative record reviewed by the project team, Planning Commission, and BOCC.

06

Third-Party Platform — Konveio

The public comment platform at elpasocountyldc.konveio.com is operated by Konveio, a private technology company headquartered in Denver, Colorado. El Paso County contracts with Konveio to host the document annotation and public comment functionality for the LDC update.

Legal Notice — Vendor Liability

El Paso County remains fully liable for the accessibility of any platform it uses to administer public programs, including platforms operated by private vendors. This is established under ADA Title II and Colorado HB 21-1110. The county cannot transfer its accessibility obligations to Konveio. If the Konveio platform fails to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards, the county is responsible for ensuring equivalent access through alternative means.

Actions Being Taken

  1. Formal notification to Konveio El Paso County has formally notified Konveio of documented WCAG 2.1 accessibility failures affecting the comment platform. Konveio has been requested to provide a remediation timeline before LDC adoption.
  2. Independent accessibility audit El Paso County is commissioning an independent WCAG 2.1 audit of both elpasocountyldc.com and elpasocountyldc.konveio.com. Results will be published in an updated version of this statement.
  3. Alternative participation paths Email submission to plnweb@elpasoco.com is available as a full equivalent alternative to the Konveio platform for all residents, regardless of disability status. Submitted comments receive identical treatment in the administrative record.
  4. Vendor contract review El Paso County is reviewing its contract with Konveio to establish WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as a contractual requirement for continued use of the platform for public participation programs.
  5. Procurement policy update El Paso County is updating its vendor procurement policy to require WCAG 2.1 AA compliance certification for any platform used to administer public participation programs before the next contract cycle.

08

Request an Accommodation

El Paso County will provide reasonable accommodations to ensure any person with a disability can fully participate in the LDC update process. Accommodations available include:

  • Meetings and hearings: ASL interpretation, real-time captioning (CART), large-print materials, accessible seating, remote participation options
  • Documents: Tagged accessible PDF, Microsoft Word format, large-print, or alternative format on request
  • Language assistance: Translation and interpretation services available on request
  • Alternative comment submission: Telephone-based comment submission available for residents unable to access written formats
  • Home or facility visits: Available on a case-by-case basis for residents with mobility limitations — contact the planning department to discuss
How to Request

Contact the ADA Coordinator at ADACompliance@elpasoco.com or 719-520-6866 at least 48 hours before any scheduled event. For document accessibility requests, contact plnweb@elpasoco.com — allow 3–5 business days for conversion to accessible format.

Requests will be accommodated to the maximum extent feasible. If a specific accommodation cannot be provided, an equivalent alternative will be arranged.

09

Enforcement and Formal Complaint

If you have raised an accessibility concern with El Paso County and are not satisfied with the response, you have the following options for formal complaint or enforcement action:

Colorado Civil Rights Division — HB 21-1110

Under Colorado House Bill 21-1110, any individual with a disability who has experienced discrimination due to inaccessible government technology may file a complaint with the Colorado Civil Rights Division or bring a civil action in Colorado court. The court will determine whether discrimination occurred and whether the government entity is liable.

  • Colorado Civil Rights Division: ccrd.colorado.gov ↗

U.S. Department of Justice — ADA Title II

Any individual may file an administrative complaint with the U.S. Department of Justice Civil Rights Division alleging that El Paso County has failed to comply with ADA Title II. DOJ complaints are investigated at no cost to the complainant. If a violation is found, remediation may be required including reopening any inaccessible public participation process.

  • DOJ ADA complaint portal: ada.gov/file-a-complaint ↗

County ADA Grievance Procedure

El Paso County maintains an internal ADA grievance procedure for resolving complaints about accessibility in county programs. Contact the ADA Coordinator to initiate this process before filing an external complaint if you wish to attempt resolution at the county level first.

Note

Filing an accessibility complaint will not affect your ability to participate in the LDC comment process or any other county program. Retaliation for accessibility complaints is prohibited under ADA Title II.
