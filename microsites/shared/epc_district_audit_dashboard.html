The real ADA gap — not the homepage

Homepage ADA is a solved problem on Streamline and state templates. The failure is document accessibility — nearly every budget, audit, and meeting minutes PDF is almost certainly untagged and fails WCAG 1.3.1. ADA Title II + HB21-1110 require tagged PDFs. Zero sites audited address this.

WSDM sites — unknown risk

30+ districts managed by WS Districts have not been audited via WAVE. Single WordPress template — if it fails, all 30+ fail simultaneously.

RESOLUTION NO. ______-2026 A RESOLUTION OF THE EL PASO COUNTY BOARD OF COUNTY COMMISSIONERS REQUIRING ALL METROPOLITAN DISTRICTS TO ESTABLISH ADA-COMPLIANT WEB PRESENCE AND PUBLISH REQUIRED GOVERNANCE DOCUMENTS BY JANUARY 31, 2027 WHEREAS, El Paso County has 284 registered metropolitan districts, of which approximately 249 (88%) have no public website as of April 2026; and WHEREAS, metropolitan districts are public entities under ADA Title II (42 U.S.C. §12132), subject to WCAG 2.1 Level AA under 28 C.F.R. Part 35, with a federal deadline of April 24, 2026; and WHEREAS, Colorado HB 21-1110 (2021) requires all Colorado government websites to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA; and WHEREAS, C.R.S. §32-1-809 requires public transparency notices; C.R.S. §32-1-202 requires accessible service plans; C.R.S. §32-1-106 requires annual audits; and WHEREAS, Colorado already provides a free, WCAG 2.1 AA-compliant state website template currently used by three EPC districts at no cost; and WHEREAS, the BOCC has oversight authority under C.R.S. §32-1-207; NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED: SECTION 1 — REQUIRED WEB PRESENCE All 284 EPC metropolitan districts shall establish a publicly accessible, WCAG 2.1 AA-compliant website by January 31, 2027, hosted on: (a) The Colorado state government website template (free); or (b) An alternative platform approved by the EPC Digital Accessibility Coordinator.
