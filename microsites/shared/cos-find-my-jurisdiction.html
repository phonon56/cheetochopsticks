Incorporated
Inside a city or town with its own government, roads, zoning, and services. Colorado Springs, Manitou Springs, Fountain, and Monument are all incorporated.
Unincorporated
Within El Paso County but not inside any city. The county governs directly — no city council, no city zoning, no city utilities. Very common in rural and exurban areas.
Jurisdiction
Which government has authority over your address. Depending on what you're asking about, two or more governments may have jurisdiction at once — over different things.
Special district
A separate unit of government that provides one specific service — water, fire, sanitation, utilities. Your address may be in several special districts at once, each with its own tax levy.
Mill levy
The property tax rate. One mill = $1 of tax per $1,000 of assessed value. Your total mill levy combines county, city/town, school district, and all special district levies.
Assessed value
The value the county assigns for tax purposes. In Colorado, residential assessed value is 6.765% of actual (market) value — set by state law, not the county.
Valuation protest
Your legal right to challenge the Assessor's value estimate. You may file every two years. Deadline: May 1st in odd-numbered years. Free. No lawyer required.
