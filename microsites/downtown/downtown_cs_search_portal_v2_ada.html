All financial data is currently missing from this portal

The records shown below are sample data only. The Downtown Partnership, BID, and DDA do not currently publish the financial documents listed below in any publicly accessible, searchable form. All items marked below are either not posted online, buried in unsearchable PDFs, or not disclosed at all — despite being legally required or expected of quasi-governmental entities spending public funds.

BID — Business Improvement District

  • Annual adopted budget for each fiscal year — not posted online
  • Audited financial statements — required by Colorado statute, not posted
  • Mill levy rate and total assessed value by parcel — not disclosed
  • Individual property assessment amounts per parcel — not posted
  • Itemized expense breakdown (clean crew, marketing, transit, admin) — not posted
  • Vendor contracts and service agreements — not posted
  • Board member names, terms, and conflict-of-interest disclosures — not posted
  • Advance meeting agendas — only minutes are posted, no pre-meeting agendas
  • BID boundary map linked to parcel data — not publicly searchable
  • Reserve fund balances — not disclosed

DDA — Downtown Development Authority

  • Annual adopted budget — not posted (required by C.R.S. 29-1-101 et seq.)
  • Audited financial statements — required by State Auditor annually, not posted online
  • Monthly financial statements reviewed at board meetings — exist but not posted separately
  • TIF reimbursement agreements — recipients, amounts, parcel addresses, terms — not posted
  • Building Enhancement Grant recipients, award amounts, and parcel addresses — not posted
  • Retail Incentive Grant recipients and award amounts — not posted
  • Tree Canopy Grant recipients and award amounts — not posted
  • Energy Efficiency Grant recipients and award amounts — not posted
  • Special Project Grant recipients and award amounts — not posted
  • Job Incentive payments — recipients, amounts, employment outcomes — not posted
  • Total TIF revenue captured year by year — not posted
  • TIF baseline assessment values and increment calculations — not posted
  • 5-mill levy collections per year — not posted
  • Total TIF committed vs. remaining capacity (TIF expires 2037) — not posted
  • List of parcels receiving TIF reimbursements linked to parcel IDs — not posted
  • Grant application outcomes — how many applied, how many denied — not posted
  • DDA boundary map linked to searchable parcel data — not posted
  • Board member conflict-of-interest disclosures — not posted
  • Advance meeting agendas — inconsistently posted

Downtown Partnership (501c4 Nonprofit)

  • IRS Form 990 (public record) — not linked on website
  • Audited financial statements — not posted
  • Executive compensation disclosure — available only via 990, not posted
  • Membership revenue total and breakdown by tier — not posted
  • Sponsorship revenue and sponsor list — not posted
  • Contract amounts paid to the Partnership by BID and DDA — not posted
  • Downtown Ventures financial statements — not posted
  • Community Ventures financial statements — not posted
  • Board member list for all four boards — inconsistently maintained

Cross-entity transparency gaps

  • No parcel-linked record index — impossible to look up a single address across all entities
  • No unified grant history — no way to see all money a building or business has ever received
  • No searchable document archive — PDFs exist but are not indexed or searchable
  • No plain-language summaries of board decisions tied to specific properties
  • No notification system for property owners or business owners in the affected districts
  • No machine-readable data exports (CSV, JSON, or API) for any financial records
  • No record of denied applications or unfunded requests
  • No status tracking for pending grants or reimbursements

How the three entities work

BID

Business Improvement District

Funded by a mandatory assessment on all property owners in the district. Covers clean and safe services, streetscaping, and events.

Funding source
Property owner mill levy (~$589K/yr)
Board
11 members appointed by City Council
Meetings
Every other month, 3rd Tuesday
Can fund
District services only — not private grants

DDA

Downtown Development Authority

Funded by a 5-mill levy plus Tax Increment Financing — the growth in property tax above a baseline, reinvested in the district.

Funding source
5-mill levy + TIF (expires 2037)
Board
11 members appointed by City Council
Meetings
Monthly, 2nd Tuesday, 9:15 a.m.
Can fund
Building grants, TIF reimbursements, housing

Partnership

Downtown Partnership

A 501(c)(4) nonprofit that staffs the BID and DDA under contract. Funded by membership dues, sponsorships, and service contracts.

Funding source
Memberships, sponsorships, contracts
Legal type
501(c)(4) — files IRS Form 990
Role
Marketing, advocacy, events, technical assistance
Note
Not subject to CORA directly; 990 is public but not linked
