Reference

What each record type is

Pick the one that matches what you need. If you're unsure, the Records Unit can help over the phone.

Police case report

A written report about a non-traffic incident. Some sections may be redacted.

Traffic accident report

Officer-completed report of a vehicle accident.

If you filed online with the state, contact Colorado State Patrol instead.

911 call or radio audio

Audio of a 911 call or police/fire radio transmission.

Kept 3 years plus the current year.

Dispatch log

Dispatcher's record of units sent to a call. Not a police report.

Background check (local)

Your CSPD arrest history only.

For statewide history, contact the Colorado Bureau of Investigation.

Body-worn camera video

Officer body-camera footage.

Redaction handled by a third-party vendor since January 23, 2026. The vendor will contact you with pricing.

Booking photograph

Requires the subject's full name and date of birth by state law.

Forensic lab photos

Photographs from the Metro Forensic Laboratory.

CSPD and El Paso County Sheriff's Office only. A separate form will be emailed.

Internal affairs investigation

CSPD employee conduct investigations.

Only investigations initiated after April 12, 2019 are releasable under CRS 24-72-303.

Good to know

Fees & your rights

Base fee

$10

Charged per request. Cost may increase based on record size or processing time. You'll be notified and can cancel before further charges are applied.

Victim's rights

Free for victims of certain crimes

If you are the victim of a qualifying crime, your report copy is free. Check the victim's rights box on the request form. Eligibility is verified by Records staff.

Colorado Springs Police

The Records Request Unit releases criminal justice records under the Colorado Criminal Justice Records Act, 24-72-301 et seq.
