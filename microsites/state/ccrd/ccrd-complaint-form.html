    What we cannot help with

    CCRD enforces civil rights in employment, housing, and public accommodations. We do not handle:

    • Criminal matters (call local police)
    • Landlord-tenant disputes unrelated to discrimination
    • Federal employment (file with EEOC)
    • Fraud or consumer issues (file with the Attorney General)

    Filing deadlines

    Most complaints must be filed within 300 days of the most recent incident under CADA. Housing complaints have a one-year deadline. If your incident is older, file anyway — exceptions may apply.

    What happens next

    • You'll receive an automated confirmation within minutes
    • An intake worker reviews within 5 business days
    • If the case is something CCRD can handle, we'll contact you to begin the formal intake
    • If not, we'll point you to the right agency

    Need help filing?

    If you cannot fill out this form yourself, you can:

    • Call 1-800-262-4845 — staff will take your complaint by phone
    • Visit any CCRD office — locations at ccrd.colorado.gov
    • Have someone else file on your behalf with your written consent

    Colorado Civil Rights Division

    A unit of the Colorado Department of Regulatory Agencies (DORA). We enforce the Colorado Anti-Discrimination Act (CADA) and serve as the state's civil rights enforcement agency for employment, housing, and public accommodations.
