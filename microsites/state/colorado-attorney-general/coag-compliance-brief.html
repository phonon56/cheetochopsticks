Mandatory Compliance Notice — Attorney General's Office of Colorado

The Colorado Attorney General's Office, as a state government entity, is legally obligated under Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act (42 U.S.C. §§ 12131–12165) and the Department of Justice's Web Accessibility Final Rule (28 C.F.R. §§ 35.200–35.205, effective June 24, 2024) to ensure that all public-facing websites, web applications, and complaint intake systems conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA by no later than April 24, 2026. The Colorado AG's office serves a population well exceeding 50,000 persons, placing it squarely within the mandatory compliance cohort specified at 28 C.F.R. § 35.200(b)(1). The DOJ has confirmed that no extensions to this deadline are available.

Enforcement of this obligation is compounded by the fact that this office is charged with enforcing ADA Title II against other Colorado entities under 42 U.S.C. § 12133. Any failure to comply with the same standards this office enforces creates substantial institutional, legal, and reputational exposure. The DOJ Civil Rights Division enforces Title II through complaint investigations, compliance reviews, and pattern-or-practice litigation. Civil penalties for a first violation are $115,231 (inflation-adjusted 2024); a second violation is $230,464. Settlement agreements with comparable state agencies have resulted in total costs of $665,000–$815,000 inclusive of remediation, legal fees, accessibility coordinator requirements, and mandatory staff training. Emergency remediation rates — applicable to work commenced after April 24 — are currently running 4–5× normal vendor rates due to market scarcity.

Additional obligations arise under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. § 794), Executive Order 13166 (LEP access — directly implicated by the Spanish-language complaint infrastructure findings), Title VI of the Civil Rights Act (42 U.S.C. § 2000d), and 8 CCR 1501-11 (Colorado Technology Accessibility Standards). The materials contained in this package constitute the good-faith compliance record recommended by DOJ guidance for entities demonstrating documented remediation effort prior to enforcement action.

Recommended Remediation Timeline

Immediately — April 7–10, 2026
Emergency fixes: broken routes, Spanish lang, skip link
Fix four broken complaint category redirections (#45–48). Add lang="es" to all Spanish page templates (#80). Move skip link to DOM position 1 (#20). Add alt text to 11 Spanish hub card images (#79). These are content/template changes — no development sprint required. Under 28 C.F.R. § 35.130(b)(1)(i), denial of program access is the highest-priority enforcement category.
Sprint 1 — April 10–17, 2026
Global CSS fixes: focus indicators, nav tab order, paragraph spacing
Add global :focus-visible CSS rule (#18). Apply tabindex="-1" to collapsed nav dropdown links (#19). Set scroll-padding-top: 40px on html (#94). Add margin-bottom: 2em to paragraph elements (#95). These are single-file CSS changes deployable in minutes.
Sprint 2 — April 17–24, 2026 (deadline)
Gravity Forms template fixes — all 7 forms simultaneously
Add aria-hidden="true" to required asterisk spans. Change Previous button type from submit to button. Add step indicators with aria-current="step". Wrap radio groups in fieldset/legend. Add role="alert" to validation container. Template-level — fix once, applies to all 7 forms.
Post-deadline — April 25 – June 2026
Salesforce platform remediation and CRM expansion
Engage Salesforce on shadow DOM label failures (#24), CSS error dialog (#11), switch toggle ARIA (#34), and progress indicator semantics (#30). Begin Salesforce Government Cloud + Service Cloud Case Management evaluation. Deploy the triage-first complaint form prototype included in this package as replacement for the current multi-step categorization flow.
Ongoing — Q3 2026 onward
Replace Siteimprove with WAVE + AI quarterly audit cycle
Cancel Siteimprove at renewal (~$28,000/year savings). Deploy Pope Tech (WAVE-powered, ~$1,200/year) for automated regression monitoring. Schedule quarterly DOM-level AI audits to catch the 60–70% of issues automated tools cannot detect. Estimated 3-year savings: $78,600, with superior coverage.

Governing Legal Authority

42 U.S.C. §§ 12131–12165
ADA Title II
Prohibits discrimination on the basis of disability in services, programs, and activities of state and local governments. Primary enforcement authority for AG website accessibility.
28 C.F.R. §§ 35.200–35.205
DOJ Web Accessibility Final Rule
Effective June 24, 2024. Adopts WCAG 2.1 Level AA as the mandatory technical standard. Mandatory compliance for entities serving 50,000+ persons by April 24, 2026. No extensions.
29 U.S.C. § 794
Section 504, Rehabilitation Act
Prohibits disability discrimination by any program receiving federal financial assistance. Applies to the AG's federally funded units (Medicaid Fraud Control, etc.).
Executive Order 13166
Limited English Proficiency Access
Requires meaningful access for LEP persons to federally funded programs. Directly implicated by the Spanish-language infrastructure findings — particularly lang="en-US" on all Spanish pages.
42 U.S.C. § 2000d
Title VI, Civil Rights Act
Prohibits discrimination based on national origin in federally assisted programs. Spanish-language nameless image links and English-only No-Call path implicate this authority.
8 CCR 1501-11
Colorado Technology Accessibility
Colorado's state-level technology accessibility standards, requiring WCAG conformance for state digital services. Independent of — and in addition to — the federal DOJ rule.
