Military effect on El Paso rate

Step-by-step: How the military-adjusted rate is calculated

All figures estimates — DoD active duty death rate ~70 per 100,000 (National Safety Council / DMDC data)

Step 1 — remove from denominator

Published population: 758,150
Subtract ~37,500 active duty: − 37,500
Civilian population: 720,650

Step 2 — remove from numerator

Published deaths: 5,516
Active duty deaths (~70/100k × 37,500): − 26
Civilian deaths: 5,490

Step 3 — civilian-adjusted crude rate

5,490 ÷ 720,650 × 100,000 = 761.8 per 100,000

This puts El Paso County's civilian rate above the Colorado statewide crude rate (~755) and approaching the US age-adjusted rate (722.0). The ~37,500 active duty military had been quietly masking a higher civilian mortality burden — a gap of 34 points per 100,000 that no public dashboard currently shows.

Section 03 · ★ Critical El Paso context

How military installations distort El Paso County mortality statistics

This is the most important section for understanding why El Paso County's numbers look the way they do. No other Colorado county has this issue at this scale.

★ The military population creates a statistical distortion in El Paso County

El Paso County contains five major military installations. Their combined active duty population is roughly 35,000–40,000 people — approximately 4–5% of the county's total population. Active duty military are among the youngest and healthiest Americans, with a median age around 22. They are included in the population denominator used to calculate death rates, but contribute far fewer deaths per capita than the civilian population. This deflates El Paso County's crude death rate and makes the county look healthier than it actually is for its civilian residents.

Fort Carson (Army) — ~26,530 active duty 3rd largest employer in Colorado. ~74,814 total population on post including families. 57% of soldiers and families live OFF-post in Colorado Springs. $2.55B annual economic impact. Source: Fort Carson Fact Sheet, Feb 2025.
Peterson & Schriever Space Force Bases — ~5,000+ active duty Peterson SFB is home to Space Operations Command. Schriever SFB hosts satellite control operations. Combined active duty and civilian workforce significant for eastern El Paso County.
US Air Force Academy — ~4,300 cadets + faculty Located in northern El Paso County. Cadets are included in census population counts. Extremely young, healthy population. Very low death rate but counted in denominator.
Cheyenne Mountain Space Force Station Smaller installation. Combined with Peterson, the Space Force presence adds several thousand more active duty and civilian personnel to El Paso County's population base.

Three categories of military deaths — how each is counted

Not all military deaths appear in CDPHE vital statistics

✓ COUNTED in El Paso 5,516

Active duty soldier stationed at Fort Carson dies in Colorado (natural causes, accident, suicide, illness). Death certificate filed with CDPHE. Listed as El Paso County resident.

✗ NOT COUNTED anywhere in CDPHE

Fort Carson soldier dies overseas in combat or a deployment. Tracked by DoD Defense Casualty Analysis System (DCAS) only. Never enters state vital statistics. El Paso County's 5,516 contains zero overseas combat deaths.

⚠ COMPLICATED — depends on residence

Soldier maintains "home of record" in Texas (for tax purposes) but lives in Colorado Springs. Death certificate uses actual residence — so they count as El Paso. If they listed Texas address, they'd count in Texas instead.

Bottom line for El Paso County analysis: The ~35,000–40,000 active duty military inflate the denominator (population) while contributing very few deaths, making El Paso's crude death rate appear lower than the civilian reality. A civilian-adjusted death rate would be noticeably higher — likely above the Colorado state average of ~654, possibly approaching the US average of 722. This calculation requires a custom data request from CDPHE to separate military from civilian deaths, or a CDC WONDER query filtering by veteran/military status.

What the 794 gap tells us: El Paso County is a regional medical hub for southern Colorado and northern New Mexico. UCHealth Memorial, Penrose-St. Francis, and military medical facilities draw critically ill patients from surrounding counties (Pueblo, Fremont, Teller, Lincoln, Crowley) and beyond. People traveling to El Paso County for better care — exactly the scenario you raised — show up in the coroner's 6,310 but not in CDPHE's 5,516. This means El Paso County's health infrastructure burden is 794 deaths larger than the resident count suggests.

Section 08 · Data gaps

Data not available in the public dashboard

Employment / occupation

Listed on death certificates but not in public CoHID. Requestable from CDPHE — would reveal military occupational mortality patterns.

Request from CDPHE ↗

Homeownership

Not collected on death certificates anywhere in the US. Cannot be requested — does not exist in this dataset.

ZIP code of residence

Exists in CDPHE records. Not in public dashboard. Allows sub-county analysis: Colorado Springs neighborhoods, Fountain, Calhan, Fort Carson CDP.

Request via email ↗

County of death vs. county of residence

Both fields in CDPHE records. Comparing them shows El Paso residents dying outside county (specialty care travel) vs. non-residents dying in El Paso facilities.

Request cross-tab ↗

Civilian-adjusted El Paso death rate

No public tool separates civilian from military population in the denominator. A civilian-adjusted rate would be meaningfully higher than the 727.6 crude rate shown here.
